Evidence-Based Research

Impact of Declining Scores

Post COVID classrooms saw a trend in declining interests. Average math scores of 8th graders in 2024 were 8 points lower than their 2019 counterparts, a record low.

"The Nation's Report Card released shows... a 3-point decrease for 12th graders on both math... since 2019, echoing declines seen in other subjects and grades after the pandemic."
The Nation Assessment Governing Board, 2025
"In both subjects [reading and writing], scores declined across all percentiles except the 90th percentile,... the highest-performing students. The achievement gap between the highest- and lowest-performing students in 12th-grade math is wider than in all previous years"
(Harris 25)

Our Vision

Conception aims to develop young students' engagement and interest in math. To us, interest is our number one priority.

"Without passion, nothing is possible and with it, the sky's the limit."

Our hope is to ignite as many students' passions for math as possible. Once students have shown genuine interest, they are more likely to do well in math in a classroom setting and feel more comfortable with math.

Whether our lessons cause someone to pursue a career in STEM or cause them to approach math with an open mind, we would consider that a success because in the end, their interests in learning will have bloomed.

Hands-on math education for the next generation of thinkers.
